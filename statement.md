Supermarket Management System - Project Documentation

Problem Statement
Traditional supermarket operations face challenges with inefficient manual inventory management, disorganized product categorization, and separate systems for customer shopping and staff management. This leads to operational inefficiencies, customer frustration in locating products, and staff difficulties in maintaining accurate product information and pricing. The current lack of an integrated system results in time-consuming processes and potential errors in billing and inventory tracking.

Scope of the Project
In-Scope
- Dual-interface system for customers and employees
- Product categorization into 5 distinct phases
- Shopping cart functionality with quantity management
- Basic inventory management (add, remove, update products)
- Receipt generation with detailed breakdown
- In-memory data storage during runtime

Out-of-Scope
- Persistent data storage between sessions
- User authentication and security
- Payment processing integration
- Inventory quantity tracking
- Multi-user concurrent access
- Advanced reporting and analytics

Target Users
Primary Users
- **Retail Customers**: Shoppers needing quick product lookup and purchase tracking
- **Store Employees**: Staff members responsible for inventory management and pricing

Secondary Users
- **Store Managers**: Monitoring overall system operation
- **System Administrators**: Maintaining application functionality

High-Level Features
Customer-Facing Features
- Product search and validation
- Shopping cart with quantity management
- Automated price calculation
- Detailed receipt generation
- Product phase location information

Employee-Facing Features
- Complete product inventory management
- Dynamic price updates
- Product addition and removal
- Real-time inventory viewing
- Category-based product organization

System Features
- Input validation and error handling
- User-friendly console interface
- Real-time calculations
- Modular and extensible architecture
